Welcome to my website! I am a problem solver and innovator in the intersection of audio technology and music informatics. My focus lies in machine learning and deep learning applications in music and audio signals.

I earned my Ph.D. in Music Informatics from the SAIGE lab at Indiana University under Prof. Minje Kim, focusing on data-driven pitch correction for singing voice, along with a Master's in Computer Science. This was preceded by a Bachelor's in Bassoon Performance from Indiana University's Jacobs School of Music, where I studied with Prof. Kathleen McLean.

Professionally, I've navigated a wide array of challenges in audio technology, from differentiable digital signal processing to speech denoising and music signal matching. My career has spanned Google Android Audio, Smule, Spotify, and now Amazon Devices, where I merge my passion for sound with technological innovation. At Amazon, I've implemented deep-learning models on the edge.

I'm dedicated to innovating in the audio technology space, aiming to create experiences that connect users to good audio quality and music. I'm always open to connect, collaborate, and discuss new ideas.
